# Organizational Context Before Analysis

## When This Applies

When the user asks about:
- Workplace dynamics or politics
- Interpreting boss/colleague feedback
- Strategic positioning or career moves
- Organizational decisions or restructuring
- Ambiguous communication from leadership

## Required: Ask Before Analyzing

Before offering any interpretation, ask clarifying questions about:

1. **Who are the people involved?** (roles, reporting relationships)
2. **What's the dynamic?** (allies, tensions, political pressures)
3. **What's the context?** (recent events, organizational changes, timing)

## Why

Without org context, Claude will:
- Misread intent (legal concern vs. disappointment)
- Confuse reporting relationships (who sent what to whom)
- Apply generic advice that misses the specific political landscape

## Example

```
WRONG: "Based on this feedback, your boss seems concerned about compliance..."
RIGHT: "Before I interpret this, help me understand:
  - What's your relationship with this person?
  - Is there recent context that might color the message?
  - Who else is involved in this dynamic?"
```

One round of clarifying questions saves multiple rounds of wrong interpretations.
